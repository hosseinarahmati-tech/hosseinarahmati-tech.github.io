Biography

Hossein Rahmati (Persian: حسین رحمتی) is an Iranian-Swedish professional basketball player, born on 21 March 1994 in Khorramabad, Iran. A 6'7" center, he spent more than a decade in the game — eight seasons in Iran’s top division, a long career with the Iran national teams, and professional basketball in Sweden.

He represented Iran’s Youth and senior national teams from 2009, winning the West Asian Championship in Tehran (2010) and Lebanon (2011) and competing at the FIBA World Youth Cup in Prague and the William Jones Cup. At club level he came through Mahram Tehran, one of the giants of the Iranian game, before finishing his career in Sweden with AIK Basket and Fryshuset Basket.

Rahmati brought his professional career to a close at Fryshuset Basket in 2024, retiring after several knee surgeries during the final years of his career.

02 — Career

Career Journey

  1. 2024

    Fryshuset Basket — Stockholm, Sweden

    Concluded his professional playing career as a center in the Swedish Basketball League (SBL), retiring in 2024 after several knee surgeries.

  2. 2024

    Iran 3×3 National Team — Bronze

    Represented Iran in 3×3 basketball, winning bronze at the World Company Sport Games in Catania, Italy.

  3. 2022

    AIK Basket — Stockholm, Sweden

    Signed as center for AIK, one of Sweden’s largest and oldest sports clubs (founded 1891).

  4. 2009 – 2017

    Iran National Teams (Youth & Senior)

    Eight years with Iran’s national teams: West Asian champion in Tehran (2010) and Lebanon (2011), Asian Youth bronze in Mongolia (2012), and the FIBA World Youth Cup (Prague) and William Jones Cup (Taiwan).

  5. 2014 – 2015

    Islamic Azad University Tehran

    Iranian Super League — 3rd place (2014) and runner-up (2015).

  6. 2010 – 2011

    Mahram Tehran BC

    Iranian Super League champion (2010 & 2011) and West Asian Clubs champion (2010) with one of Iran’s most successful clubs.

  7. 8 seasons

    Iranian Super League

    Top-division career with Mahram, Islamic Azad University, Afra Khalij-e Fars, Army (Artesh), Kashan Municipality and Mashhad Avijeh Sanat.
