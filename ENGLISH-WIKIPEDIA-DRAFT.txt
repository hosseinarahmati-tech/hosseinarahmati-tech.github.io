'''Hossein Rahmati''' ({{lang-fa|حسین رحمتی}}; born 21 March 1994) is an Iranian
professional [[basketball]] player who plays as a center for Fryshuset Basket in the
[[Basketligan|Swedish Basketball League]]. He has been a member of the [[Iran men's national
basketball team|Iran national team]] set-ups since 2009 and spent eight seasons in the
[[Iranian Basketball Super League]] before continuing his career in Sweden.<ref name="fiba"/><ref name="eurobasket"/>

== Early life ==
Rahmati was born on 21 March 1994 in [[Khorramabad]], Iran.<ref name="fiba"/>

== Club career ==
=== Iran ===
Rahmati began his senior career with [[Mahram Tehran BC|Mahram Tehran]], one of the most
successful clubs in Iranian basketball, where he won the Iranian Super League in 2010 and
2011 and the West Asian club championship in 2010.<ref name="superleague1011"/><ref name="waba2010"/>
He subsequently played in the Iranian Super League for Afra Khalij-e Fars, Azad University
Tehran, the Army (Artesh) team, Kashan Municipality, Avizheh Sanat Arak and Avizhe Sanat
Parsa Mashhad, totalling eight seasons in Iran's top division.<ref name="eurobasket"/><ref name="asiabasket"/>
With Azad University Tehran he finished third in the Super League in 2014 and runner-up in 2015.<ref name="cn1">[URL NEEDED — Iranian sports news, e.g. Varzesh3/ISNA]</ref>

=== Sweden ===
Rahmati moved to Sweden, where he has played in the Swedish Basketball League. He represented
[[AIK Basket]] in Stockholm in 2022 before playing for Fryshuset Basket.<ref name="aik">[URL NEEDED — aikbasket.se official announcement]</ref><ref name="realgm"/>

== National team ==
Rahmati has represented Iran at youth and senior level since 2009.<ref name="isna"/> With the
national team he won the West Asian Championship in Tehran (2010) and in Lebanon (2011),
took bronze at the Asian Youth Championship in Mongolia (2012), and competed at the FIBA
World Youth Championship in Prague (2012) and the [[William Jones Cup]] in Chinese Taipei
(2014 and 2017).<ref name="cn2">[URL NEEDED — federation / Iranian sports news]</ref>
In 2024 he played 3x3 basketball for Iran, winning a bronze medal at the World Company Sport
Games in [[Catania]], Italy.<ref name="cn3">[URL NEEDED — event organiser or news]</ref>
